Notably, freedom to express imaginings of the future contrasted between the Soviet Union and the United States largely due differences in media control. Soviet media was overlooked by the governing party, giving an influence to the public of more positive imagining and with more emphasis on societal goals in messagings. While the media was largely controlled in the United States under the initial wave of McCarthyism and the hayes codes prior, artists were given more liberty to express narratives for public view. Alike to the Soviet Union, moere public narratives did fall into positive narratives under the 1950s McCarthyism. As the century progressed, more critical narratives of imagined dystopias would fill American pop culture through the allowed freedom of expression via publications and studios.
